how do I kill the entire script if it's running in the background with many worker processes running and queued?                                               
                                                                                                                                                                     
● The worker processes are spawned children — killing just the parent leaves them running. Kill the entire process group instead:                                    
                                                                                                                                                                     
  # Kill everything in the process group (parent + all workers + wandb sidecars)                                                                                     
  kill -- -$(pgrep -f run_production_sweep)                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                     
  The - before the PID means "kill process group". Since the workers are spawned children they inherit the same PGID as the parent.                                  

  If it doesn't die (e.g. a worker is stuck in C code):
  kill -9 -- -$(pgrep -f run_production_sweep)

  You can verify everything is gone with:
  pgrep -fa run_production_sweep
  ps aux | grep golobs | grep python | grep -v grep
