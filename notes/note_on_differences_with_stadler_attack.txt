The core mechanism is essentially identical — both train a classifier on the synthetic dataset using QI columns as features and  
  predict the hidden attribute(s). The meaningful differences are:                                                                 
                                                                                                                                   
  1. Target records                                                                                                                
  Stadler infers attributes for arbitrary target individuals (drawn from the population, not necessarily in training). Your attack 
  runs inference on the actual training records (and a disjoint holdout). This is a deliberate design choice — you're measuring how
   much the SDG memorizes specific individuals, not just whether it preserves population-level correlations.                       
                                                                                                                                   
  2. The limitation Stadler identifies — which your memorization test directly addresses                                           
  Stadler explicitly flags that their attack can infer attributes even for people not in the training set, making it impossible to 
  tell whether the leakage is from record-level memorization vs. population-level correlation. Their attack can't answer that      
  question. Your RA_delta_mean (train accuracy minus holdout accuracy) is specifically designed to measure exactly this: does the  
  SDG leak more about training records than it does about population-level base rates?

  3. Single vs. multiple hidden features
  Stadler targets one sensitive attribute at a time. Your framework reconstructs all hidden features simultaneously (or
  sequentially via chaining).

  4. Survey scope
  Stadler uses their attack to demonstrate a vulnerability exists. Your framework sweeps across 9+ SDG methods, multiple epsilon
  values, and attack variants to characterize how much each method leaks.

  In short: You're running essentially Stadler's attack, but answering the harder follow-up question they left open — by using a
  disjoint holdout to separate memorization from correlation leakage.
