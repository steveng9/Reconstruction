Why chaining fails and what you haven't tried                                                                
                                                                                                               
  Iterative refinement (Gibbs-style): Your current chaining is a single left-to-right pass. The deeper variant 
  is to run multiple passes: predict all features once (any order), then loop back and re-predict each feature 
  conditioning on the current best guesses for all others. Repeat until stable. This turns the linear cascade  
  into coordinate-wise optimization over the hidden features. Error propagation is still an issue on the first 
  pass, but subsequent passes can correct early errors. This is the natural counterpart to your oracle         
  experiment and is cheap to implement on top of your existing chaining infrastructure.                      
                                                                                                               
  Multi-output (all-at-once) prediction: You already mention this as a TODO — one model predicting all hidden  
  features simultaneously. This captures inter-feature dependencies without sequential error accumulation. A   
  single MLP/LightGBM with multi-output heads is the canonical version. This is worth at least one result in   
  the section since it's a clean alternative to chaining.                                                      
                                                                                                               
  The SDG-interaction experiment you noted is genuinely publishable: MST encodes only pairwise marginals, so   
  conditioning on a predicted feature adds essentially nothing beyond what QI already captures — the synth     
  joint distribution doesn't have it. TabDDPM and AIM should show larger chaining gains. This is a concrete,   
  testable claim with a clean interpretation: chaining's effectiveness is bounded by the fidelity of the joint 
  synthetic distribution. Even a small table showing chaining gain vs. SDG method would be a novel finding   
  worth including.

  ---
  Why ensembling fails and what you haven't tried
                                                 
  Stacking / meta-learning: Your current approach is soft voting (averaging posterior probas). Stacking uses a 
  meta-model that learns when to trust which base model from held-out predictions. The meta-model's training   
  signal is: "for records with these QI values, RandomForest is right and MLP is wrong." This is more          
  expressive than averaging. Generate out-of-fold base-model predictions on the synth data, train a lightweight
   meta-model (logistic regression or shallow RF), then apply to targets. This is worth trying because your    
  oracle-vs-individual gap (11.4 points) is large — the information exists somewhere.                        
                                                                                                               
  Diversity through QI subsampling: All your current base models use the full QI. Training different models on 
  different QI subsets forces diversity in a structural way, not just in model class. Models with complementary
   QI views may fail on different records.                                                                     
                                                                                                               
  ---                                                                                                        
  Other enhancements reviewers are likely to request
                                                    
  Bayesian network inference from synth: Fit a BN structure directly to the synthetic data and compute exact   
  conditionals. This is a natural alternative to ML classifiers — instead of learning P(hidden | QI)           
  discriminatively, you're reading it off the joint model. For categorical data this is particularly clean.    
  Reviewers who work in PGMs will ask about this.                                                              
                                                                                                               
  Confidence-based abstention: Rather than predicting on all records, abstain on records where no model has    
  high confidence. Optimize precision over recall. This reframes the attack as "how many records can we        
  reconstruct with high confidence?" which may be more relevant to the privacy auditor's actual question.      
                                                                                                               
  Using multiple synth releases: If the threat model allows the attacker to query the SDG multiple times       
  (multiple independent synthetic datasets from the same training data), each release is an independent draw — 
  averaging predictions across releases should reduce variance. This is analogous to test-time ensembling via  
  data rather than models.                                                                                     
                                                                                                             
  ---
  What I'd write in the paper
                             
  Your negative result is real and publishable. The honest framing is:                                         
                                                                                                               
  - Soft voting fails because ensemble diversity is insufficient — attacks share the same QI signal, and       
  failure correlates on the hardest records regardless of model class.                                         
  - Chaining fails because error propagation outweighs information gain — and the gain is further bounded by   
  how much high-order joint structure the SDG preserves.                                                       
  - The oracle gaps (11.4 for ensembling, 7.2 for chaining) show there is headroom in principle, making these  
  negative results informative rather than trivial.                                                            
                                                                                                               
  The SDG-interaction experiment for chaining would strengthen the paper significantly — it converts "chaining 
  didn't work" into "chaining's benefit scales with SDG fidelity," which is an actionable, interpretable       
  finding.
