  The Variables Worth Isolating                                                                                
                                                                                                               
  Section 4.3 wants to answer: how does the adversary's prior knowledge shape reconstruction risk? There are   
  really three orthogonal questions:                                                                           
                                                                                                               
  Q1 — QI size: As the adversary knows more features, does RA increase monotonically? Does it saturate?        
                                                                                                               
  Q2 — QI composition (same size): Does it matter which features are known — demographic vs. behavioral,       
  high-cardinality vs. binary, correlated with hidden vs. not?                                                 
                                                                                                               
  Q3 — Hidden feature difficulty: When QI changes, does reconstruction difficulty shift uniformly across hidden
   features, or do some features become newly easy/hard?                                                       
                                                                                                               
  Q3 is almost free if you already have per-feature RA scores — which you now do via the new script.           
                                                                                                               
  ---                                                                                                          
  Why You Don't Need New Synthetic Data                                                                        
                                                                                                               
  Critically: changing the QI does not require re-generating synth.csv. The synthetic data contains all        
  features. You're just changing which columns are "known" at attack time. So the cost here is just re-running 
  the attacks, not SDG generation.

  ---                                                                                                        
  What to Skip
              
  - Don't vary QI on California — it only has 9 features and all are continuous, making QI composition         
  uninteresting (no categorical/continuous contrast in the QI itself).                                         
  - Don't try to fully cross all combinations. The story doesn't need it. Two datasets × (size sweep + one     
  composition contrast) is sufficient.                                                                         
  - Skip QI variants that put high-cardinality features like fnlwgt (28k values) or capital-gain (123 values)  
  in the hidden set — the scoring gets noisy and the narrative becomes harder to interpret.                    
                                                                                                               
  ---                                                                                                          
  What the Experiments Would Produce                                                                           
                                                                                                               
  1. RA-vs-QI-size curve (per attack, on adult): does it saturate? Is there a "knee"? Do different attacks     
  saturate at different points?                                                                                
  2. Composition contrast table (QI1 vs QI_behavioral, matched size): does RA change when composition shifts?  
  Which attacks are sensitive to this?                                                                         
  3. Per-feature RA breakdown (using the new script): when you grow QI from 3→6→10, which hidden features      
  become easier to reconstruct? This would show that high-cardinality or QI-correlated features are the "first 
  to fall."                                                                                                    
  4. DP interaction: does the RA-vs-ε curve look different under a weak vs. strong QI? This is potentially the 
  most policy-relevant finding — DP may protect more when the adversary knows less.                            
                                                                                                               
  ---                                                                                                          
  My Recommendation for Minimum Viable Experiment                                                              
                                                                                                               
  If compute time is the binding constraint, I'd run in this priority order:                                   
                                                                                                               
  1. Adult QI_tiny + QI_large (you already have QI1) → 3-point size curve, ~2 attack × 3 QI × all SDG methods  
  2. Adult QI_behavioral → composition contrast with QI1 (same SDG data, just different attack config)         
  3. CDC QI_tiny + QI_large → replication in health context                                                    
                                                                                                               
  The behavioral QI variants are essentially free since they reuse the same synth.csv and just need new entries
   in get_data.py.
