  On the metric requirement: I was right the first time — both attacks do require querying a privacy metrics API. That's
  actually central to their argument: the vulnerability is in the metrics themselves leaking information, not the synthetic data
   release per se. So the threat model is fundamentally different from yours.                                                   
                                                                                                                                
  On outlier definition: They fit a GMM on the synthetic data and identify records that fall in the smallest/most isolated      
  clusters. The logic is: outliers are hard to model accurately by the generator, so they tend to appear distinctively when the 
  generator does produce them.
